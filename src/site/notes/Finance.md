**What is Finance?**
> Finance is an applied field _within_ economics, focusing on how individuals, businesses, and governments manage, create, and study money, banking, credit, and investments, while economics studies the broader systems of production, distribution, and consumption of resources, making finance the practical application of economic theory to financial decision-making under uncertainty. ==Essentially, economics asks "how does the whole system work?", and finance asks "how do we manage and grow money within that system?"==.

### Valuation


#### Sharpe Ratio
> financial metric that measures the risk-adjusted return of an investment portfolio by dividing its excess return (above the risk-free rate) by its standard deviation

"bang for your buck" regarding **risk**. Or even "efficiency", like miles-per gallon, where miles are your return and gallons are your risk.

how much **extra profit** you earn for every unit of **uncertainty (volatility)** you accept. A high ratio means you are being well-compensated for the danger of losing money, while a low ratio suggests you are taking on high risks for meager rewards. It helps you decide if a portfolio's gains are due to **smart investing** or just **reckless gambling**.
##### Formula
(return - risk-free rate) / std dev

Investors often use these benchmarks to judge performance:
- **< 1.0**: Sub-optimal/Poor.
- **1.0 - 1.99**: Acceptable/Good.
- **2.0 - 2.99**: Very Good/Great.
- **> 3.0**: Excellen
