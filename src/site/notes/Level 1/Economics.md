**Microeconomics** studies small-scale decisions (individuals, firms, specific markets) on supply, demand, and prices, like a microscope on individual trees, 

while **macroeconomics** examines the big picture (nations, global) concerning GDP, inflation, unemployment, and growth, like studying the whole forest, with both interconnected as individual choices drive large-scale trends.

Key Differences

- **Ownership:** Private (market) vs. Public/State (command).
- **Decision-Making:** Individuals/Businesses (market) vs. Central Authority (command).
- **Resource Allocation:** Supply & Demand (market) vs. Government Planning (command).
